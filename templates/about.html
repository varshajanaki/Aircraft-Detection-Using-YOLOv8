About the Project

Model Training

This aircraft detection system is built using YOLOv8, a state-of-the-art object detection model. YOLOv8 (You Only Look Once) is known for its speed and accuracy in real-time object detection tasks.

The model was trained on a comprehensive dataset of military aircraft images, with 43 different classes of aircraft. The training process involved optimizing the model to recognize various aircraft types from different angles and in different lighting conditions.

What do these metrics mean?

  • Precision: The ratio of correctly predicted positive observations to the total predicted positives. High precision means low false positive rate.
  • Recall: The ratio of correctly predicted positive observations to all observations in actual class. High recall means low false negative rate.
  • mAP50: Mean Average Precision with IoU threshold of 0.5. This measures how well the model detects objects with at least 50% overlap with ground truth.
  • mAP50-95: Mean Average Precision averaged over multiple IoU thresholds (from 0.5 to 0.95). This is a more comprehensive metric that evaluates detection quality across different overlap requirements.
